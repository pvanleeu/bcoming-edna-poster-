Sampling protocolsHow the eDNA and bat captures were collected in the field

The eDNA substrates and bat-trapping protocols used across the BCOMING field campaigns. In 2024 (Stung Treng, methods-benchmark) all five terrestrial substrates were sampled in parallel at three karst sites. In 2025 (cross-sectional, Phnom Penh · Battambang · Stung Treng) the team applied the best-performing trio — spider webs, leaf swabs and hair traps — plus water samples. Performance highlights from the GLMM analyses are in bold.

Overview of the BCOMING sampling design across the two campaigns.

🕸️ Spider-web swabs

2024 · 2025

Orb spider webs sampled within the same 10 × 10 m plots as leaf swabs. In 2024, dedicated 3-minute swabs of orb webs in NAP buffer. In 2025, pooled with leaf swabs on the same swab head — three spider webs sampled per swab, in addition to the leaves. Webs passively trap eDNA from passing vertebrates — particularly bats — and significantly outperformed every other terrestrial substrate in the methods benchmark.

🐭 Hair traps

2024 · 2025

40 baited PVC tubes per site (5–8 cm diameter, ~5 m apart) with three bait types (peanut-butter banana · sweet potato · corn). Rebaited every 1–2 days, retrieved after ≥ 6 days. Hair collected and stored in ethanol. Best substrate for rodents.

🍃 Leaf swabs

2024 · 2025

Three sterile swabs per 10 × 10 m plot, 3 minutes each on leaves and bark. In 2025, each swab was pooled with spider-web sampling — three webs per swab in addition to the leaves — to halve field effort while preserving signal. Targets eDNA deposited by perching, brushing or roosting vertebrates. Best substrate for birds.

💧 Water sampling (aquatic eDNA)

2025 only · Cross-sectional

Surface water collected at several spots around each water point using a sterile sampling bag (Nature Metrics aquatic kit), then pushed manually through a Sterivex-type 0.8 μm in-line syringe filter until the filter saturates. Filter sealed and preserved with stabilising buffer. Captures the aquatic vertebrate community that terrestrial substrates miss — fishes, amphibians, and any vertebrate visiting the water (incl. wildlife / livestock co-occurrences).

💨 Air filters

2024 only · Method-validation

Three tripods × three samplers per site, 7-hour sessions starting at dusk, in parallel with bat trapping. Filters trap airborne eDNA from flying and locally active vertebrates; samples handled with gloves and masks, stored dry in UV-sterilised bags.

🟫 Surface soil

2024 only · Method-validation

Nine sub-samples pooled within each 10 × 10 m sub-site plot, collected with Nature Metrics kits at sites of recent animal activity (tracks, dung, dust baths) and preserved with NAP buffer. Lowest amplification success of the five substrates (< 50 %).

🦇 Bat trapping (mist-netting)

2024 · longitudinal

Mist nets deployed at cave entrances and along putative flyways at dusk, across three non-consecutive nights per site. Each captured bat is measured, photographed, identified morphologically by an expert and confirmed by DNA barcoding when needed, and swabbed (rectal swab for sarbecovirus screening, fecal for diet and gut-microbiome) before release at the capture site. Linked work: longitudinal microbiome–virus study (preprint).

Marker performanceStung Treng 2024 · methods benchmark

Three metabarcoding markers were amplified from every sample. 12S delivered the highest number of detections overall, but the two CO1 markers resolved a higher proportion of detections to species level — illustrating the trade-off between detection volume and taxonomic resolution.

Sampling effort by eDNA substrateStung Treng 2024 · methods benchmark

152 terrestrial eDNA samples were collected across five substrates over six days at three karst sites. After PCR amplification and quality filtering, 125 samples were retained. Soil yielded the lowest success rate (under 50 % amplification), reinforcing that substrate choice strongly shapes what the sequencer ever sees.
